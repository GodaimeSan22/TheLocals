Young Irelander THOMAS DAVIS
The Patriot

The Voice of a Nation

Born in Mallow in 1814, Thomas Davis was the chief organizer and soul of the Young Ireland movement. Educated at Trinity College Dublin, he sought to unite all Irishmen, regardless of creed, under a banner of national identity and cultural pride.

"Educate that you may be free... A nation without its language is only half a nation."

His legacy lives on through "The Nation" newspaper and his stirring ballads like "A Nation Once Again", which remain pillars of Irish patriotism.
